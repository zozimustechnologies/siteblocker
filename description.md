# Site Blocker — Block Distracting Websites

Take control of your browsing. Site Blocker lets you block up to 100 distracting websites with a clean, easy-to-use side panel — right inside Microsoft Edge.

## Why Site Blocker?

Whether you're a parent keeping your kids safe online, a student staying focused during exams, or a professional cutting out distractions — Site Blocker gives you simple, reliable website blocking that works instantly.

## Key Features

🛡️ **Block up to 100 websites** — Add any domain and it's blocked immediately, including all subdomains.

🔄 **Individual toggle controls** — Temporarily pause blocking for any site without removing it from your list.

⚡ **Master switch** — Turn all blocking on or off with a single click. Your list is always preserved.

🔒 **Child safety math challenge** — An optional math question gate prevents children from modifying the blocklist. Parents solve a quick math problem to access settings; kids can't get through. Easily toggle this on or off from the Settings page.

⚙️ **Settings page** — Configure extension behavior via the cog icon in the side panel header.

🌐 **Subdomain blocking** — Blocking `facebook.com` automatically blocks `m.facebook.com`, `www.facebook.com`, and all other subdomains.

🎯 **Side panel interface** — A modern, clean interface that opens in the Edge sidebar. No disruptive popups.

🚫 **Clear blocked page** — When you visit a blocked site, you see a straightforward "Site Blocked" message with a Close Tab button.

## Permissions Explained

| Permission | Why it's needed |
|---|---|
| Storage | Save your blocklist locally in your browser |
| Side Panel | Display the side panel interface |
| Declarative Net Request | Block and redirect websites at the network level |
| All URLs | Apply your blocking rules to any website you choose to block |

## How to Use

1. Click the Site Blocker icon in your Edge toolbar to open the side panel
2. Type a domain (e.g., `reddit.com`, `x.com`, `tiktok.com`) and click **+ Add**
3. The site is blocked instantly — including all subdomains
4. Use individual toggles to pause/resume blocking per site
5. Use the master switch to pause all blocking at once
